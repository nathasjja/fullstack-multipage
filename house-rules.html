MAIN HOUSE RULES

  1. Keys - Keep the house locked when no one is home. Never share information about the house or its members with outsiders. Like they say in the movie fight club. Rule #1: You do not talk about fight club.
  2. Noise - Keep noise to a minimum after 11:00 PM. We're already a bit on the edge, so let's keep the peace.
  3. Guests - Always inform host about bringing guests who are not part of the household. This includes day visitors and overnight guests.
  4. Shared Spaces - Clean up after yourself and respect others' privacy. This includes keeping common areas tidy and not accessing personal items without permission - like personal journals, wardrobe, storage spaces, etc....
  5. Cats - Respect the cats' space and needs. Always check their body language before interacting with them.
  6. Personal Items - Always ask before using someone else's belongings, and return them in the condition you found them.
  7. Mail - Respect the privacy of others' mail. Don't open or read anything that isn't addressed to you.

PEACE

  • Resolve conflicts collectively: Disagreements are part of growth; we use dialogue and mediation, not dominance.
  • Protect the sanctuary: This house is a refuge from harm, exploitation, and coercion

LOVE

  • Care is a duty: We look after one another’s health, safety, and dignity as much as our own.
  • Generosity over scarcity.: Share food, tools, and time. When one of us eats, we all eat..

Unity

  • We build together, not alone: No one carries the load in silence.
  • Solidarity is non-negotiable: When one of us struggles, the rest lean in.

Respect

  • Respect labor: Every contribution counts - whether it’s sweeping the floor, writing in the collective journal, or cooking a meal.
  • Respect the commons: Clean what you use, replace what you finish, leave the world better than you found it.

Socialist Grounding

  • From each according to ability, to each according to need. Workload is distributed fairly, but compassion trumps rigidity.
  • Collective decisions. Major choices - new members, events, house policies, finances - are made by group consensus.
  • Reject hierarchy. Roles exist for function, not for status; nobody’s voice outweighs another’s.

Community before commodity. No buying and selling within the house; exchange is based on trust, care, and mutual aid.
